Mountain Adventure · Tanzania

Mount Kilimanjaro

Africa's rooftop — 5,895 metres of rainforest, moorland, alpine desert and equatorial ice. No technical climbing required, just heart, legs and our expert crew.

Highlight

The Roof of Africa

Kilimanjaro is the highest free-standing mountain on Earth and the only place where you can walk from tropical farmland to arctic glaciers in less than a week.

Our KPAP-partnered crews — professional guides, cooks and porters — maintain some of the highest summit success rates on the mountain, built on sensible acclimatisation, hearty food and constant health checks.

Highlight

Choose Your Route to the Summit

The scenic Machame route, the classic hut-to-hut Marangu route, or the quiet, beautiful Lemosho traverse — each offers a different balance of scenery, comfort and acclimatisation.

Whichever you choose, summit night is unforgettable: a slow head-torch climb under a river of stars, arriving at Uhuru Peak as the sunrise sets the glaciers on fire.

Best Time to Visit & What to Expect

Everything you need to know before you go — timed and tailored by our local experts.

Best Time to Visit

Jan – Mar

Warm & Clear

Warm days, quiet trails and the chance of snow on the crater rim — a beautiful, less-crowded window.

Jun – Oct

Peak Trekking Season

The driest, most stable weather of the year with superb summit visibility. The most popular months on every route.

Full Moon

Summit by Moonlight

Time your climb with the full moon and ascend the final slopes with the glaciers glowing silver around you.

What to Expect

World-class mountain crew

Licensed guides, twice-daily health checks, emergency oxygen and rescue protocols on every climb.

Quality camps & meals

Roomy tents, thick sleeping mats and hot, high-energy meals cooked fresh on the mountain.

Honest acclimatisation

Routes and pacing designed 'pole pole' — slowly, slowly — to get you to the top safely.

A summit certificate

And a memory that will outlast every photograph: sunrise from the Roof of Africa.
