Amboseli National Park

Great tuskers striding beneath the snows of Kilimanjaro — Africa's most iconic photograph, waiting for you to take it.

Highlight

Elephants Beneath the Mountain

Amboseli's elephants are research-famous — studied for over 50 years and carrying some of the largest tusks left in Africa. They parade daily between the dusty plains and emerald swamps fed by Kilimanjaro's melting snows.

Position yourself at dawn as the clouds lift off the summit, and capture the photograph every wildlife photographer dreams of.

Highlight

Swamps, Dust & Superb Light

The park is a study in contrasts: mirage-shimmering dry lakebeds, papyrus swamps crowded with hippo and buffalo, and dust-devils dancing between wandering herds.

Climb Observation Hill for a 360° panorama, then spend golden hour among the elephants as Kilimanjaro blushes pink behind them.

Best Time to Visit & What to Expect

Everything you need to know before you go — timed and tailored by our local experts.

Best Time to Visit

Jun – Oct

Dry Season

Wildlife crowds the swamps, dust makes the light magical and the mountain views are at their most reliable.

Jan – Feb

Short Dry Window

Clear skies, lush-ish plains and superb photography between the rains.

Early A.M.

Mountain Hours

Kilimanjaro usually shows herself at dawn and dusk — we plan every drive around her moods.
