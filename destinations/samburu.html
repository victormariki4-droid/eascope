Samburu Reserve

Kenya's wild north — a semi-desert oasis along the Ewaso Ng'iro River, home to rare northern species and the proud Samburu people.

Highlight

The Samburu Special Five

Samburu rewrites your checklist: Grevy's zebra with pin-stripe coats, reticulated giraffe like living stained glass, blue-necked Somali ostrich, spiral-horned beisa oryx and the impossibly long-necked gerenuk standing tall to browse.

None of them occur in Kenya's southern parks — this is a genuinely different safari.

Highlight

Life Along the Ewaso Ng'iro

In this thirsty land, everything comes to the river: elephants digging wells in the sand, leopards lounging in riverine figs, lion prides patrolling the doum palms.

Beyond the wildlife, the Samburu people — 'butterflies of the north' in their beaded finery — share a culture as vivid as the land itself.
