Serengeti National Park

Endless golden plains where over two million wildebeest, zebra and gazelle thunder across the horizon — the greatest wildlife show on Earth, and it never stops.

Highlight

The Great Wildebeest Migration

Nothing on Earth compares to it. Each year more than two million animals move in a great clockwise circle around the Serengeti ecosystem — calving in the southern plains from January to March, then surging north to brave the crocodile-filled Grumeti and Mara rivers between June and September.

Our guides read the herds daily and position you exactly where the drama unfolds — river crossings, predator chases, and newborn calves taking their first steps.

Highlight

Big Cats & Endless Plains

The Serengeti holds Africa's highest concentration of large predators — some 3,000 lions, plus leopards lounging in sausage trees and cheetahs scanning the plains from termite mounds.

Add a dawn hot-air balloon flight drifting silently over the awakening savannah, followed by a champagne bush breakfast, and you have the safari of a lifetime.

Best Time to Visit & What to Expect

Everything you need to know before you go — timed and tailored by our local experts.

Best Time to Visit

Jun – Oct

Dry Season — Prime Game Viewing

Grass is short, animals crowd the rivers, and the migration masses in the north. The famous Mara River crossings peak July–September.

Jan – Mar

Calving Season

Half a million wildebeest calves are born in the southern plains in just weeks — and the predators know it. Extraordinary action, fewer crowds.

Nov – Dec

Green Season

Short rains transform the plains into emerald green. Superb bird life, dramatic skies and excellent value rates.
