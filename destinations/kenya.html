About Kenya

The Cradle of Safari

Kenya is where the word "safari" was born. From the world-famous Masai Mara Reserve — scene of the dramatic wildebeest crossing — to the elephant-studded plains below Kilimanjaro in Amboseli, the flamingo-fringed shores of Lake Nakuru, and the remote wilderness of Tsavo, Kenya is a land of spectacular contrasts and extraordinary wildlife encounters.

Top Attractions

Kenya's Best Destinations

From the Mara River crossings to the pink lakes — adventure at every turn.

National Reserve

Masai Mara Reserve

Africa's greatest wildlife spectacle. Witness the dramatic wildebeest river crossings from July to October, with lions, cheetahs, leopards, and elephants thriving year-round in this iconic reserve.

Discover More
National Park

Amboseli National Park

Africa's most iconic photo opportunity — vast elephant herds silhouetted against the snow-capped peak of Kilimanjaro. A paradise for photographers and wildlife lovers alike.

Discover More
National Park

Lake Nakuru

Famous for its vast flocks of flamingos turning the lake shore pink. Also home to rhinos (both black and white), lions, leopards, and hundreds of bird species in the Rift Valley.

Discover More
National Park

Tsavo East & West

Kenya's largest national park — vast, remote and wild. Home to the famous red elephants, the Mzima Springs, Lugard Falls, and incredible diversity of birds and mammals.

Discover More
National Reserve

Samburu Reserve

North Kenya's hidden gem — home to rare species found nowhere else: Grevy's zebra, reticulated giraffe, Beisa oryx, and Somali ostrich alongside the resident lion prides.

Discover More
City & Culture

Nairobi City Tours

Africa's most vibrant capital — visit the Nairobi National Park (with city skyline views!), the David Sheldrick Elephant Orphanage, Karen Blixen Museum, and the Giraffe Centre.
