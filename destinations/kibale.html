Primate Trekking · Uganda

Kibale Forest — Chimpanzees

The primate capital of the world — thirteen species in one forest, led by over 1,500 chimpanzees whose hoots shake the canopy.

Highlight

Trekking the Chimpanzees

Follow the whoops and drumming buttress-roots into the forest until you find them: chimpanzees grooming, squabbling, cracking nuts and swinging overhead — our closest living relatives at home.

Kibale's habituated communities give you an exceptional success rate, and the Chimpanzee Habituation Experience lets serious enthusiasts spend a full day with the apes.

Plan This Experience
Highlight

Thirteen Primates & the Bigodi Wetland

Beyond the chimps, Kibale is a primate carnival — red colobus, L'Hoest's monkeys, grey-cheeked mangabeys and the nocturnal potto among them.

The community-run Bigodi Wetland Walk adds swamp boardwalks, 200 bird species and village encounters where your fees fund local schools directly.
