Queen Elizabeth National Park

Tree-climbing lions, hippo-crowded channels and volcanic crater lakes — Uganda's most visited park packs astonishing variety between two Rift Valley lakes.

Highlight

The Kazinga Channel Cruise

The boat safari along the Kazinga Channel is one of Africa's great wildlife experiences: hundreds of hippos grunting in the shallows, elephants swimming between banks, buffalo wallowing beside crocodiles, and skies busy with kingfishers and fish eagles.

It is game viewing at water level — relaxed, close and endlessly photogenic.

Highlight

The Tree-Climbing Lions of Ishasha

In the park's remote southern sector, lions do something lions aren't supposed to do: they climb giant fig trees and drape themselves over the branches to sleep away the heat.

Add crater-lake viewpoints, salt-lake flamingos and chimp tracking in the Kyambura Gorge, and Queen Elizabeth becomes a full safari in a single park.

Best Time to Visit & What to Expect

Everything you need to know before you go — timed and tailored by our local experts.

Best Time to Visit

Jun – Aug

Dry Season

Short grass and busy waterholes — prime time for the Kazinga Channel and Ishasha's lions.

Dec – Feb

Second Dry Window

Hot, clear days and superb boat-cruise conditions.

Year-round

Equatorial Green

Straddling the Equator, the park stays lush and rewarding in every month.

What to Expect

Lions in the fig trees

Ishasha's famous climbers — one of only two such populations in Africa.

Africa's best boat safari

The Kazinga Channel's hippo megapods and swimming elephants.

Chimps in a sunken gorge

Track chimpanzees through the 'Valley of Apes' at Kyambura.

Crater-lake panoramas

Explosion craters and salt lakes straight from a lost world.
