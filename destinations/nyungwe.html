Nyungwe Forest National Park

One of Africa's oldest rainforests — chimpanzees at dawn, thirteen primate species, and a canopy walkway swaying 70 metres above the ferns.

Highlight

The Canopy Walkway

Step onto East Africa's only canopy walkway and cross a 160-metre suspension bridge hung 70 metres above the forest floor — eye level with turacos, hornbills and acrobatic monkeys.

Below and around you stretches a rainforest that has stood for at least 25,000 years, one of the most biodiverse places on the continent.

Plan This Experience
Highlight

Chimps & the Great Colobus Troops

Rise before dawn to track chimpanzees through the mist as the forest wakes around you, or find Nyungwe's celebrity primates — Angola colobus troops several hundred strong, streaming through the canopy like black-and-white banners.

With 13 primate species and over 300 birds (29 found nowhere else but these mountains), every trail delivers.
