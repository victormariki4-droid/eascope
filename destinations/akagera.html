Akagera National Park

Africa's greatest conservation comeback — a Big Five savannah of lakes, papyrus and rolling hills, reborn on Rwanda's eastern border.

Highlight

The Comeback Park

Twenty years ago Akagera had lost its lions and rhinos entirely. Today, after one of Africa's boldest restoration projects, both are back, populations are booming, and the park stands proudly among the continent's Big Five destinations.

Game drives roll through a gorgeous mosaic of acacia savannah, papyrus swamp and lake-dotted lowlands unlike anywhere else in East Africa.

Highlight

Lakes, Boats & Birdlife

Take a sunset boat safari on Lake Ihema past grunting hippo pods, basking crocodiles and African fish eagles calling from the palms.

With over 500 bird species — including the elusive shoebill and the papyrus gonolek — Akagera pairs its Big Five badge with world-class birding.
