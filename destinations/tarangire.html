Tarangire National Park

Ancient baobabs, python-draped sausage trees and elephant herds three hundred strong — Tanzania's best-kept safari secret, just two hours from Arusha.

Highlight

Land of Giants

Tarangire shelters some of the largest elephant herds in Africa — families a hundred strong digging for water in the dry riverbed, bathing, sparring and shepherding wobbly calves past your vehicle.

Above them tower the baobabs: two-thousand-year-old giants whose bottle-shaped trunks store thousands of litres of water and shade lion prides through the midday heat.

Highlight

The Dry Season Miracle

When the plains outside dry up, the Tarangire River keeps flowing — and the wildlife pours in. Zebra, wildebeest, buffalo, oryx and eland crowd the water alongside 550 recorded bird species.

With a fraction of the Serengeti's visitors, you'll often have a leopard, a lion pride or an elephant crossing entirely to yourself.

Best Time to Visit & What to Expect

Everything you need to know before you go — timed and tailored by our local experts.

Best Time to Visit

Jun – Oct

Dry Season — The Prime Months

The river becomes a wildlife magnet and elephant numbers peak. Simply one of the best game-viewing experiences in Africa.

Nov – Dec

Short Rains

The park turns green, migratory birds arrive in their thousands and the light is superb for photography.

Jan – Mar

Quiet Season

Warm days, low visitor numbers and beautiful landscapes dotted with new-born wildlife.

What to Expect

Elephants like nowhere else

Herds of 100+ are common in the dry season — often at arm's length from the track.

Iconic baobab scenery

The most photogenic trees in Africa, glowing gold at sunrise and sunset.

Superb birding

Over 550 species, from yellow-collared lovebirds to martial eagles.

Fewer vehicles, more wilderness

A wilder, quieter alternative — or perfect companion — to the northern circuit giants.
