Gorilla Trekking · Uganda

Bwindi Impenetrable Forest

An ancient rainforest sheltering nearly half the world's mountain gorillas — one hour with a wild gorilla family will change you forever.

Highlight

One Hour With the Gorillas

After a trek through mist and tangled vines, your guide parts the leaves — and there they are: a silverback the size of a small car, mothers cradling infants, juveniles tumbling through the branches.

For one strictly-protected hour you share their world, close enough to hear them breathe. Travellers consistently call it the single most moving wildlife encounter on Earth.

Highlight

The Impenetrable Forest

Bwindi is a 25,000-year-old UNESCO World Heritage rainforest, draped over steep valleys and ridgelines, alive with 350 bird species, 200 butterflies and 120 mammals.

Between treks, walk community trails with the Batwa people, the forest's original inhabitants, and let Uganda's famous warmth win you over.

Best Time to Visit & What to Expect

Everything you need to know before you go — timed and tailored by our local experts.

Best Time to Visit

Jun – Aug

Prime Trekking

Drier trails, clearer skies — the most comfortable window for the forest's steep paths.

Dec – Feb

Second Dry Season

Excellent trekking conditions and slightly easier permit availability.

Book Early

Permits Are Limited

Only 8 visitors per gorilla family per day — we secure your permits months ahead.

What to Expect

A guaranteed-effort encounter

Trackers locate the families at dawn; success rates are near 98%.

Expert trek support

Guides, trackers and optional porters who make the steep forest accessible to most fitness levels.

Strict, ethical viewing

One hour, seven metres, small groups — rules that keep the gorillas wild and safe.

Batwa cultural encounters

Meet the forest's first people and hear the forest through their stories.
